Pitkin County Housing Strategy

Project Overview

Pitkin County partnered with

SHG Advisors to develop a comprehensive Housing Strategy that responds to rising demand, shifting community needs, and long-term affordability challenges.

Our role was to bring clarity to a complex issue—using data, community insight, and strategic frameworks to help county leaders understand current gaps and define the most effective path forward. The result: a shared plan that supports coordinated action, stronger decisions, and a sustainable housing system for the future.

The Challenge

Pitkin County faces a unique and evolving set of housing pressures:

We use a collaborative, evidence-driven approach that ensures each solution is practical, tailored, and grounded in real-world insight.

  • Limited availability of affordable units for year-round residents
  • Workforce displacement and rising demand for local housing
  • Fragmented policies and programs across agencies
  • Fragmented policies and programs across agencies
  • Lack of consistent data to guide long-term decisions

Findings and Measurable Results

Key Insights
  • Housing challenges are connected across incomes, sectors, and regions.
  • Current programs work but lack shared metrics to measure progress.
  • Better alignment among agencies can significantly boost impact.
  • Workforce stability relies on consistent, accessible housing options.
  • Long-term planning must reflect seasonal population and economic shifts.
Common results include:
  • A data-informed, community-driven housing strategy
  • A data-informed, community-driven housing strategy
  • Better alignment across agencies and partners
  • Tools that strengthen decisions, resources, and evaluation
  • Shared indicators that make progress transparent and trackable.

The Pitkin County Housing Strategy ensures that future decisions are grounded in equity, data, and long-term sustainability, helping the region thrive for years to come.
